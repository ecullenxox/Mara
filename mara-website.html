Our mission

Science that moves people

Est. 2025 — Ireland

Ireland has one of the richest and least understood marine environments in Europe. Our coastal waters are home to basking sharks, vast kelp forests, ancient maerl beds, and seagrass meadows that rival tropical reefs in biodiversity.

Most people will never see them. That's the problem we're here to solve.

"The ocean off our coasts is not empty. It is one of the most extraordinary ecosystems on the planet — and almost nobody knows it exists."

Mara brings together marine scientists, Irish artists, and coastal communities to create work that is scientifically rigorous, visually beautiful, and genuinely useful for conservation. Every piece we make tells a real story about a real place.

Field guide

Species of note

Irish coastal ecosystems
Basking Shark
Cetorhinus maximus
The world's second-largest fish visits Irish waters each summer. A filter feeder on plankton, utterly harmless, and one of the ocean's most magnificent animals.
Vulnerable
Maerl
Phymatolithon calcareum
Ancient coralline algae that grows just 1mm per year. Maerl beds are among the most biodiverse habitats in Ireland, housing hundreds of species.
Endangered
Common Dolphin
Delphinus delphis
Ireland's most frequently sighted cetacean. Travels in superpods of hundreds. The west coast is one of Europe's premier dolphin-watching locations.
Protected
Native Oyster
Ostrea edulis
Once so abundant they fed Irish coastal communities for millennia. Now functionally extinct in most of their range. Restoration projects are slowly bringing them back.
